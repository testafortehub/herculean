Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

1. Overview

Herculean ("we," "us," "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard information when you use our Service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Search Queries & Responses

Your search queries and the responses generated by AI models are processed in real-time to provide the Service. Herculean does not store, log, or retain your search queries on our servers. Queries are transmitted directly to third-party LLM providers (see Section 4).

2.2 Local Storage Data

Your browser's local storage tracks daily search count to enforce the 5-search free tier limit. This data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to Herculean servers.

2.3 Analytics & Event Data

We use PostHog to collect anonymized analytics events such as:

  • Feature usage (search initiated, synthesis generated)
  • Error rates and performance metrics
  • Device type and browser information
  • Aggregated session duration

PostHog does not collect your search query text. Event data is anonymized and cannot be tied back to you personally.

2.4 Server Logs

Standard web server logs may record your IP address, request timestamp, and HTTP status. These logs are retained for security and troubleshooting purposes and are deleted after 30 days.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Service Delivery: Processing and delivering search results from multiple AI models
  • Analytics: Understanding feature usage, performance, and user engagement via PostHog
  • Security: Detecting and preventing abuse, fraud, and unauthorized access
  • Service Improvement: Identifying bugs, optimizing performance, and enhancing user experience

4. Third-Party Services & Data Sharing

To provide the Service, Herculean transmits your search queries to the following third-party AI providers:

  • OpenAI (GPT-4o-mini) — via OpenAI API
  • Google (Gemini 2.0) — via OpenRouter
  • Anthropic (Claude 3 Haiku) — via OpenRouter
  • Groq (Llama 3.1) — via Groq API
  • Cerebras (Llama 3.1) — via Cerebras API
  • OpenRouter (Mistral, Qwen, Nemotron) — for model aggregation
  • PostHog — for anonymized analytics

Each provider has its own privacy policy and data practices. Herculean is not responsible for their handling of data. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies at their respective websites.

5. Data Retention

  • Search Queries: Not retained by Herculean; subject to each third-party provider's policy
  • Server Logs: Deleted after 30 days
  • Analytics Events: Retained by PostHog according to their retention policy (typically 12 months)
  • Local Storage: Persists on your device until cleared by you

6. Cookies & Tracking

Herculean uses minimal cookies. PostHog analytics may place cookies to track sessions. You can disable analytics cookies by blocking PostHog in your browser or adjusting privacy settings. Disabling analytics will not prevent use of the Service.

7. User Rights & GDPR

If you are located in the European Union or have other privacy rights under applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data Herculean holds about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Restrict processing of your data
  • Data portability

To exercise these rights, contact us at the address below. Herculean does not retain search query data, so GDPR deletion requests are limited to PostHog analytics data.

8. Children's Privacy

Herculean is not intended for users under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware of such collection, we will delete it promptly.

9. Security

We employ industry-standard security measures including SSL/TLS encryption, secure API communication, and regular security audits. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Significant changes will be announced via email or a prominent notice on the Service. Your continued use constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
